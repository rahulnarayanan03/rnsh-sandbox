Product Overview

One Clear Workflow from Test Selection to Results.

Aeris brings the complete respiratory muscle strength assessment into one portable product, guiding the clinician from test selection and patient instructions through repeated attempts and results.

Maximum Inspiratory Pressure measures the strength produced while breathing in, while Maximum Expiratory Pressure measures the strength produced while breathing out. Together, these tests help clinicians assess respiratory muscle weakness linked to chronic lung disease and neuromuscular disorders.

Each test guides the clinician through repeated breathing efforts, shows the pressure curve as it happens, and checks when three results are close enough to be considered repeatable. The final summary brings the result, predicted comparison, repeatability, attempt count, and test time together on one screen.

Client

Developed for Royal North Shore Hospital.

The Respiratory Investigation Unit at RNSH asked for a more accessible way to measure respiratory muscle strength than systems costing over $75,000.

Optik turned the brief into Aeris, a portable product that guides MIP and MEP tests, records each attempt, and presents clear results.

Designed for respiratory laboratories, speech pathology, and pulmonary rehabilitation.

Project Brief

The Core Requirements for Aeris.

01

Make testing more accessible

Create a portable alternative that reduces reliance on expensive laboratory systems and makes respiratory muscle strength testing easier to access.

02

Support MIP and MEP testing

Guide clinicians through the two tests used to assess the strength generated while a patient breathes in and breathes out.

03

Show each attempt clearly

Present the pressure curve during each breathing effort so clinicians can review how the test was performed rather than relying on one final number alone.

04

Build in repeatability checks

Compare repeated attempts and clearly show when three results are within the required 10% range, helping clinicians judge whether the test is consistent.

05

Prepare for clinical validation

Create a working prototype that can be compared with established laboratory equipment before it is considered for clinical use.

06

Fit the clinical workflow

Make the instructions, navigation, repeated attempts, and results clear for respiratory laboratories, speech pathology, and pulmonary rehabilitation teams.

Development Process

From Clinical Need to Working Prototype.

1
Stage 1Understanding the clinical workflow

The team worked from the RNSH brief to define what clinicians need before, during, and after a MIP or MEP test: clear instructions, repeated attempts, repeatability guidance, and an easy-to-read result summary.

2
Stage 2Shaping the product experience

The complete journey was mapped from choosing a test to reviewing the final result. This established what the clinician should see, what action comes next, and how Aeris should respond at each stage.

3
Stage 3Building the guided testing flow

Separate MIP and MEP journeys were created with clear test selection, step-by-step instructions, live test feedback, repeated attempts, and a consistent path to the final results.

4
Stage 4Refining results and repeatability

The results experience was refined to show the breathing effort, compare repeated attempts, and make the repeatability outcome easy to understand without searching across multiple screens.

5
Stage 5Testing the experience and preparing the next stage

The physical prototype and browser-based simulator were used together to refine the instructions, navigation, pressure curve, and result layout. The next stage is formal comparison with established laboratory equipment before clinical use.

Final Product

The Complete MIP and MEP Testing Workflow.

01

Clear test selection and instructions

The clinician selects MIP or MEP, follows instructions written for that test, and moves through the same clear sequence for each repeated breathing effort.

02

Live view of each breathing effort

Each attempt is shown as a pressure curve, making it easier to review the shape and strength of the effort. The workflow can be controlled by touch or physical buttons.

03

Automatic repeatability guidance

Aeris compares repeated attempts and clearly shows when three results meet the 10% repeatability criterion, helping the clinician know when the test is consistent.

04

One clear result summary

The final screen brings the measured result, predicted comparison, repeatability, attempt count, and test date and time together in one place.
