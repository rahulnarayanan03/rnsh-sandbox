Product Overview

The Core Test, Without the Usual Cost.

Laboratory systems for breathing strength testing can cost over $75,000, while portable alternatives can still cost around $3,000. Aeris is designed to bring the essential testing experience into one portable product at a much lower price point.

Maximum Inspiratory Pressure (MIP) measures breathing-in strength, while Maximum Expiratory Pressure (MEP) measures breathing-out strength. Aeris guides both tests through the same clear process, from choosing the test and following instructions to completing repeated tries and reviewing the final reading.

A live graph shows each breathing effort as it happens. Aeris compares repeated tries and tells the clinician when the readings are close enough together to be considered consistent. The final screen shows the reading, how it compares with expected values, the number of tries, and the test time.

Client

Built Around a Real Clinical Need.

The Respiratory Investigation Unit at Royal North Shore Hospital asked for a more accessible way to test breathing muscle strength without relying entirely on laboratory systems costing over $75,000.

Aeris turns that brief into one portable system for breathing-in and breathing-out strength tests, guided tries, a live graph, and clear results.

Designed for respiratory laboratories, speech pathology clinics, and breathing rehabilitation programs.

Development Process

From a Costly Test to a More Accessible Product.

1
Stage 1Start with the clinical need

The team defined what clinicians need before, during, and after each breathing-in or breathing-out strength test, including clear instructions, repeated tries, help to confirm a reliable result, and an easy-to-read summary.

2
Stage 2Make every step clear

The journey was shaped from choosing a test to reviewing the final reading, keeping each action clear and the next step easy to understand.

3
Stage 3Bring both tests into one system

The breathing-in and breathing-out strength tests were brought into one consistent experience with clear choices, guided tries, live feedback, and a direct path to the final reading.

4
Stage 4Turn repeated tries into reliable results

The result view was refined to show each effort, compare three tries, and make it obvious when the readings are close enough together.

5
Stage 5Prepare Aeris for hospital comparison

The complete experience was refined across the physical product and simulator, preparing Aeris to be checked against standard hospital lab equipment.
